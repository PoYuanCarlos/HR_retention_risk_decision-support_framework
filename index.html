GADA Capstone Portfolio Extension

Workforce Retention Risk Analytics

A predictive and decision-support framework for identifying employee attrition risk, overwork patterns, promotion gaps, and actionable HR retention priorities.

HR Analytics Predictive Modeling Decision Support PACE Workflow

Project Positioning

This project extends a standard course capstone into a portfolio-ready business analytics case. The goal is not only to predict whether employees may leave, but also to translate model outputs into retention strategy, risk segmentation, and stakeholder-facing recommendations.

01 / Project Summary

From model assignment to HR decision-support case

The original GADA capstone asks analysts to explore employee data and build a model predicting whether an employee will leave the company. This portfolio version reframes the same analytical foundation as a workforce risk management problem for HR stakeholders.

The project emphasizes the full business-to-analytics chain: problem framing, data quality review, exploratory analysis, model construction, model interpretation, ethical use, and practical retention recommendations.

Core Business Question

How can HR identify workforce risk patterns early enough to improve employee retention, reduce overwork-related attrition, and prioritize managerial interventions?

Primary Outcome

A classification-based risk framework that supports HR decisions rather than simply labeling employees.

02 / Portfolio Value Add

What makes this more than a course submission?

Business Decision Layer

Adds stakeholder questions, HR use cases, retention KPIs, and decision pathways beyond model scores.

Risk Segmentation

Converts predicted attrition probability into Low, Moderate, High, and Critical risk groups.

Responsible Analytics

Addresses data leakage, employee labeling risk, fairness concerns, and misuse of predictive outputs.

Executive Communication

Prepares model outputs for Tableau dashboard design, management interpretation, and one-page summary use.

03 / Method

PACE workflow adapted for portfolio presentation

P

Plan — Problem Framing

Define HR stakeholders, turnover cost, business questions, target variable, and decision-support goals.

A

Analyze — EDA and Data Quality Review

Check missing values, duplicated records, outliers, class balance, workload patterns, satisfaction trends, and tenure effects.

C

Construct — Predictive Model and Feature Engineering

Compare logistic regression and tree-based models; test feature leakage concerns; create interpretable risk features such as overworked status.

E

Execute — Recommendations and Decision Support

Translate feature importance, probability scores, and risk groups into HR retention actions, policy review, and management communication.

04 / KPI Design

Suggested HR analytics KPIs

01

Attrition Rate

Employees who left / total employees.

02

Overwork Rate

Employees above the monthly workload threshold / total employees.

03

Project Overload Rate

Employees assigned to six or more projects / total employees.

04

Promotion Gap Rate

Employees without recent promotion / total employees.

05

High-Risk Share

Employees classified as High or Critical risk / total employees.

06

Retention Priority Score

Weighted indicator combining workload, tenure, promotion, and model risk.

07 / Responsible Analytics

Ethical use boundary

Do not punish predicted risk

The model should support retention, workload review, and employee assistance—not punitive targeting.

Check leakage-sensitive variables

Satisfaction and evaluation scores may not always be available or may reflect processes already tied to attrition.

Review fairness patterns

Risk results should be compared across salary, department, and tenure groups to avoid structural bias amplification.

Communicate limitations

Predictions should be treated as decision-support signals, not definitive claims about individual employee intent.
